SuperIsland Chrome Bridge — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-25

The SuperIsland Chrome Bridge extension connects your Chrome tabs to the SuperIsland macOS app so the app can show which of your in-progress tasks need you and take you back to the exact tab. This policy explains what the extension accesses and how that data is used.

What the extension accesses

  • Tab information — the URL, title, and identity of your open tabs.
  • Page content — a lightweight summary of the current page's visible text and structure, used to detect whether a task on the page is working, finished, or waiting on you (for example, an AI assistant that has stopped and is asking a question).
  • Navigation events — when tabs change, so status can update.

How the data is used

The extension sends this information over Chrome's native messaging to the SuperIsland app running on the same computer. The app uses it to update task status and to refocus the right tab.

When a page's status can't be determined locally, a short text summary of the page may be sent to SuperIsland's classification service (hosted on Supabase) and on to Anthropic's API to determine the task's status. Only the minimal text needed for that judgment is sent.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell or share your data with third parties for advertising.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking inside the extension.
  • We do not store your browsing history. Page summaries are processed transiently to determine status and are not retained beyond operational logs.

Permissions

The extension requests access to all sites (<all_urls>) and to tabs because the tasks you track can live on any website, and status detection needs to read the active page. The extension only acts on tabs you are using with SuperIsland.
